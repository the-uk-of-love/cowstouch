Vedic & Traditional Texts

References to the healing, purifying, and therapeutic properties of cow products from ancient scriptures and Ayurvedic classics.

  • 1
    Charaka Samhita (c. 3rd–2nd century BCE)

    Describes cow milk, ghee, curd, urine, and dung as balancing the three doshas, supporting digestion, immunity, and longevity; Panchagavya formulations for detox and rejuvenation.

  • 2
    Sushruta Samhita

    Details use of ghee for wound healing, gomutra (cow urine) as antimicrobial and bioenhancer, and gomaya (cow dung) as antiseptic and skin remedy.

  • 3
    Ashtanga Hridaya

    Classifies Gomutra as ushna (hot), katu (pungent), and rasayana (rejuvenating); prescribes Panchagavya in Panchakarma therapy.

  • 4
    Agni Purana (292.10–13)

    States cow urine, dung, milk, curd, and ghee purify body and spirit; recommends Govrata for health and well-being.

  • 5
    Manusmriti (11.91–92)

    Lists the five cow-products as sacred purifiers and therapeutic agents.

  • 6
    Parashara Smriti (11.28)

    Declares Panchagavya inherently pure and purifying.

  • 7
    Bhavaprakasha Nighantu

    Notes Gomutra balances digestion, reduces inflammation, treats skin, respiratory, and metabolic disorders.

  • 8
    Shivambu Kalpa

    Ancient treatise on therapeutic use of urine, including cow urine, for healing and detoxification.

  • 9
    Vrikshayurveda

    Prescribes Panchagavya for soil health, plant immunity, and natural pest control.

  • 10
    Bhagavad-gītā & Śrīmad-Bhāgavatam

    Teach go-rakṣya (cow protection) as a core spiritual principle; emphasize the cow's sacred status and connection to health, prosperity, and purity.
